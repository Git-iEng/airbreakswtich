Reliable Isolation with Air Break Switches (ABS) Disconnectors

The Air Break Switch (ABS) is a crucial component in all overhead power transmission lines , primarily used to connect or disconnect (on/off switching) specific sections of overhead lines and transformers from the main distribution network.

This feature enables users to isolate a faulty segment of the wire without disrupting the remaining power transmission system , making the isolation process simple, reliable, and effective for maintenance and protection.

Discover Our Comprehensive Solutions

Air Break Switches are essential for overhead transmission and distribution networks. They provide on/off switching and sectionalizing to isolate specific line segments or transformers without interrupting the wider system supporting fast fault clearance, safe maintenance and operational flexibility.

We design and supply gang-operated triple-pole ABS for outdoor applications, suitable for switching transformers, overhead lines and cables on distribution systems. Learn more: ieng.tech/air-break-switches.

Purpose & Operation

Air Break Switch Disconnectors enable isolation of a defined overhead line section through a sequence of switching operations without disrupting the rest of the network.

  • Sectionalize feeders and isolate faults quickly
  • Provide visible isolation points for safe work
  • Facilitate maintenance and network reconfiguration

Construction & Actuation

Standard designs are triple-pole, gang-operated for synchronized phase operation in outdoor environments.

  • Manual operation via handle/ratchet or insulated hook-stick
  • Outdoor duty: substation yard, pole-top, or pad-mounted enclosures
  • Suited for transformers, overhead lines and cable switching

Types of Air Break Switches

Available as single-pole and gang-operated three-pole configurations to match system design and operating practice.

  • Single-Pole ABS (independent pole control)
  • Gang-Operated ABS (simultaneous 3-phase switching)

Mounting & Installation

Configurable to suit network topology and available structure types.

  • Vertical or horizontal mounting
  • Pole-top, substation structure or pad-mounted steel enclosure
  • Outdoor application as the primary use case

Voltage & Current Ratings

Rated for medium-voltage distribution systems with ample current capacity for feeder and transformer switching.

  • Voltage: 12 kV to 36 kV
  • Current: up to 1250 A

Materials & Insulation

Built for durability and dielectric performance in outdoor conditions.

  • Ceramic, polymeric, porcelain, and cycloaliphatic components
  • Corrosion-resistant metalwork and hardware

Applications

Used wherever switching or isolation of MV circuits is required.

  • Between cables and transformers; transformers and conductors
  • Power generation export lines and distribution feeders
  • Critical points for rapid fault isolation

Benefits

Improve safety, reliability and maintainability of the network.

  • Visible isolation; supports safe work practices
  • Rapid sectionalizing stabilizes the network after faults
  • Overcurrent/short-circuit protection coordination
  • High reliability, low maintenance, easy serviceability

Air Break Switch Supply Process

At iEngineering, we ensure the supply of high-reliability **Air Break Switches (ABS)** and **Load Break Switches (LBS)** for high-voltage distribution systems. Our consolidated 6-step process focuses on accurate technical consultation, guaranteed quality through stringent testing, and seamless delivery to maintain the functional integrity of your electrical network. We cover everything from initial specifications to final site integration.

  1. 1. Technical Specification & Review

  2. 2. Design, Production & QA

    Manufacturing of ABS/LBS units using certified, durable materials. Comprehensive **Quality Assurance (QA)** checks are performed throughout the production phase.

  3. 3. Factory Acceptance Test (FAT)

    Units undergo full electrical and mechanical FAT, including insulation and operational tests, ensuring peak performance and readiness for dispatch.

  4. 4. Logistics & Delivery

    Final system integration, protection testing (if applicable), and energization to bring the Air Break Switches into full operational readiness.
